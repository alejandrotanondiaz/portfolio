Phase Two: Final Design

Design Process & Design Decisions

I decided to focus on the question regarding the increase in population as I think it had the most interesting results. Additionally, I decided to use a line graph instead of a bar chart to show more clearly which group had a higher increase.

Reflection

The question I decided to tacke was how the increase of population looked in different age groups. I decided to split the graph into male and female such that it could be apparent how the increase by sex shifts as they get older. In both datasets, there are more women than men in the later years but the increase of older men is way more drastic than the increse of older women.

To make this visualization, I had to create a new metric in the data regarding percentage increase. To calculate this, I divided the 2000 number of people over the 1900 number of people and multiplied by 100 to get the percentage value. I decided to choose the colors green and brown to label the sexes because I did not want to use color that are traditionally used for sexes and could perpetuate unintended stereotypes and perceptions into the visualization. I also labeled the lines in the graph itself to make it easier for the reader to understand what they meant.

This final design is a combinations of the previous sketches I had made in phase 1. I liked how the multiple lines in sketch 1 differentiated majority between male and female. Additionally, I choose which question to tackle based on sketch 3 and teh intriguing trend that can be seen on it.
