Final Reflection

For this exercise, I wanted to use climate change as it is one of our most pressing issues and one that has led to throwing around culpability between different actors. I also wanted to integrate some ways China is vilified by the mainstream media in the United States. Based on this, I knew I wanted to play with total versus per capita measurements and how we can use these measurements to transmit a desired message. It was really interesting how the same information can be presented to provoke different emotions. Also, it was intriguing and a bit worrisome to discover how statistical concepts like total vs per capita, which serve a specific purpose can be coopted and weaponized for something they are not meant to be used for.

I think a persuasive choice is one that stays true to the utility of the measurements being used but maybe tries to amplify the trend but always in a manner that is in line with an analysis using the intended statistical purpose. A misleading choice, however, is one in which a certain message is amplified with visual aids but the substance of the visual aid is not necessarily aligned with the premise that is being promoted but is close enough for the reader to be convinced.
